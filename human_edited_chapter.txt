Dick hurried to the cliff edge, but as he reached it, the water shimmered like a mirage and disappeared. He tumbled into the lagoon, the memory of Marua flickering in his mind.

Tari called from the shore, his small voice carried by the wind: “Taori, the fish!”

Catafa stood still, her eyes fixed on the horizon where Palm Tree Island had briefly appeared—ghostlike and full of threat. The Melanesians, fierce and relentless, believed they could one day seize Karolin for themselves.

Dick turned, fire in his chest. War would come. But today, he had time—and a tribe to prepare.
